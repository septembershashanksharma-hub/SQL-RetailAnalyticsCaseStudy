# SQL-RetailAnalyticsCaseStudy

Here’s a clarified summary of the Retail Analytics case study:


1. Business Goals:
A retail company wants to use data analytics to fix slow growth, poor customer engagement, and inventory issues.


2. Main Problems:
Product Performance: Hard to know which products sell well or poorly.
Example:* If Product X sells 200 units/month and Product Y just 5, marketing and inventory decisions should differ.
Customer Segmentation: No clear way to group customers for targeted marketing.
Example:* Customers buying less than 10 items could get discounts to encourage more orders.
Customer Behavior: Not enough info on repeat buying or loyalty patterns.
Example:* Identifying customers who buy monthly enables loyalty rewards.


3. Steps & Techniques:
Data Cleaning & EDA using SQL: Ensure data is accurate before analysis.
Example:* Remove duplicate sales records or fix missing prices.
Product Sales Analysis: Find best/worst-selling products.
Customer Segmentation: Categorize customers by purchase volume:
No Orders: 0 purchases
Low: 1–10 purchases
Mid: 10–30 purchases
High Value: >30 purchases
Example:* Customer A buys 2 items = Low; Customer B buys 35 = High Value.
Behavior Analysis: Look for patterns like repeat buys.
Example:* Count how many times a customer has shopped in a year.


4. Data Structure:
Use tables: Sales Transaction, Customer Profiles, and Product Inventory.

Summary:
This structured type of analysis helps retailers understand sales, group customers for personalized marketing, manage inventory better, and boost loyalty by focusing on real buying behaviors.
